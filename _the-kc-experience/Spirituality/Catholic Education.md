<p><strong><u>Spirituality Programme in KC</u></strong>
</p>
<p>CHIJ Katong Convent’s spirituality programme offers a suite of contemplative
practices and experiences to ground and center everyone regardless of her
faith. To develop communion, connection, and awareness of God, self and
others, the school makes room for the following:&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>School Ethos inspired by Founder</strong>
</p>
<p>Blessed Barrè’s beliefs are shared by the school leaders at the start
of each school year and during induction for new staff:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>The self-worth and dignity of every person.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Each child is unique and is blessed with God-given talents.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>We can nurture each child to realize her potential and develop her sense
of duty &amp; responsibility to the community.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Annual Theme based on Scriptures</strong>
</p>
<p>The theme is shared at meetings, school assemblies and programmes.&nbsp;
For 2026, the theme is “Trust in the Lord and do good.” (Psalm 37:3)</p>
<p>The beliefs and theme undergird how members of KC care, learn and interact
with one another, guided by God’s words and Christian values.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Morning Reflection</strong>
</p>
<p>Morning reflection is a daily occurrence in KC, with students and staff,
regardless of faith, coming forward to share their experiences and learning
with the rest of the school. The reflections and sharing follow a thematic
approach designed by the school chaplaincy team to focus on faith, IJ identity
and mission, and values.&nbsp;&nbsp;As part of this reflection, there is
also a short sharing of the Gospel. In line with the Church’s liturgical
calendar,&nbsp;this year being Year A, the Gospel readings are taken from
the Gospel of Matthew, helping us reflect on Jesus’ teachings and how they
guide our daily lives.&nbsp;At the end of each morning reflection is a
prayer, led by a Catholic student, inviting the whole school community
to pause, reflect, and begin the day with gratitude and purpose.</p>
<p><strong>Mid-Day Prayer</strong>
</p>
<p>Mid-day prayer takes place daily at 11.50 a.m. where the whole school
pauses to listen to a scripture reading, a hymn or a prayer.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Eucharistic Celebrations</strong>
</p>
<p>Masses are celebrated monthly (where possible) and according to the liturgical
calendar. There are also masses that are held in conjunction with key milestones
in the school year such as Beginning of Year Mass, Founder’s Day Mass,
Teachers’ Day Mass and End of Year Mass.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>CCE Lessons</strong>
</p>
<p>The CCE resources are enhanced to highlight Christian values and Catholic
Social Teachings where appropriate.&nbsp;</p>

<br><strong><u>Catholic Education in KC</u></strong>
</p>
<p>The Catholic education programme falls under the main responsibility of
the School Chaplaincy Team (SCT). The Catholic teachers play an important
role in ensuring that Catholic students receive a holistic spiritual education
in the school through its programmes, activities and formal lessons, in
alignment with the Catholic churches’ vision for its Catholic mission schools.
As part of our own faith journey, staff participate in a retreat organized
by our sister school (KCP) and sessions by ACCS and IJ where relevant.</p>
<p><strong>Prayer Services</strong>
</p>
<p>Catholic students are invited to attend weekly prayer sessions in the
Chapel to instill a sense of eternity through the need to connect themselves
with God. These sessions are led by the Catholic Support Leaders (CSLs)
and held on Wednesdays from 7:15-7:30 a.m.</p>
<p><strong>Catholic Connect</strong>
</p>
<p>This is a weekly programme to establish a Catholic student community rooted
in faith through fellowship and being immersed in the Gospel. This is led
by members of the Young Christian Student (YCS) Movement and facilitated
by the Catholic Support Leaders and parent volunteers. Sessions are held
every Thursday from 7:35-8:35 a.m. This is the main platform through which
the Gospels of St Mark and St Luke are taught to the students.</p>
<p><strong>Catholic Education Sunday</strong>
</p>
<p>As part of our service to the community, together with our sisters from
CHIJ Katong (Primary), Catholic Support Leaders in KC serve at Catholic
Education Sunday mass as part of the choir, hospitality and lector teams.</p>
<p><strong>Retreats for Catholic students</strong>
</p>
<p>Conducted at the end of the year to deepen faith and camaraderie. Retreats
may be conducted by the Catholic teachers or by external Catholic trainers.
